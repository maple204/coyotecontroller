User Guide

Getting started

  1. Click Connect Primary or Connect Secondary — a picker will appear listing available Bluetooth Coyote devices and USB audio outputs (DACs). Primary controls CH1 / CH2; Secondary controls CH3 / CH4.
  2. Set a Max limit for each channel you plan to use. Output is safety‑gated: no signal starts until at least one channel Max is above 0%.
  3. Bring up Master Volume slowly. Use STOP to immediately set Master to 0 and center all cursors.
  4. Optionally toggle Monitor to hear a preview mix through your speakers or headphones.

Connecting devices

  • Bluetooth (DG-LAB Coyote V2/V3) — select from the browser’s Bluetooth picker. Channels pulse sequentially (never simultaneously).
  • USB DAC / audio output — select any audio output device. The app synthesises a stereo signal: Left = odd channel, Right = even channel. Primary slot drives CH1 (L) and CH2 (R); Secondary drives CH3 (L) and CH4 (R).

DAC settings

  • Waveform — choose the carrier shape sent to the DAC: Sine, Triangle, Square, or Sawtooth.
  • S‑D Compensation — applies a Strength‑Duration curve correction so higher frequencies feel consistent in intensity. Leave on for most devices; turn off if your device already applies DSP internally.
  • Phase & Phase Cycle controls are only shown when a DAC slot is active. They offset or continuously rotate a channel’s phase — useful for creating stereo sweep effects between the two channels of a pair.

Channel controls

  • Amplitude — the per-channel trim, bounded by that channel’s Max safety cap.
  • Frequency Multiplier — scales Base Frequency independently per channel.
  • Phase — static offset relative to the other channels (DAC only).
  • Phase Cycle — continuously rotates phase over time (DAC only).

Touch / Vis pad

  • Controls two virtual sticks: one biases frequency & phase, the other biases amplitude.
  • Drag toward a corner to emphasise that channel. Release to ease back to centre.

Motion scripts

  • Select a script and press Start to animate the pad automatically through parametric or keyframe paths.
  • Script Intensity scales how far the motion reaches — keep Max caps conservative.

Biome mode

Switch to Mode: Biome to enter an autonomous deep-sea ecosystem. Four Parasites (the coloured rings) hunt creatures and latch on — each latched Parasite drives one audio channel based on that creature’s unique sensation character.

  • Jellyfish — slow rolling ocean swells (wave)
  • Radiolarians — lub‑dub heartbeat double‑pulse
  • Siphonophores — smooth silky continuous stroke
  • Polychaetes — deep rhythmic power thrust
  • Nudibranchs — liquid double‑harmonic swell
  • Glass Squids — rapid electric buzz with AM envelope
  • Ctenophores — expanding concentric ring ripples
  • Mantis Shrimp — quick‑quick‑STRONG gallop punch
  • Pyrosomes — very slow tease that climbs but never fully releases
  • Sea Angels — delicate paired wing‑beat flutter with rest

ADSR drift: each individual creature slowly evolves its own envelope timing over time, so no two of the same species ever feel identical.

Caretaker

  • The Caretaker feeds nutrients into the ecosystem, shaping which species thrive and therefore which sensations dominate.
  • Press Start to activate the selected Caretaker; press again to stop.
  • Press 🎲 Random to enter Random mode — the app automatically switches to a different Caretaker every 1–3 minutes, keeping the experience unpredictable. Press again to stop.
  • Caretaker Influence controls how frequently nutrients are dropped — higher = faster ecosystem shifts.
  • Six Caretakers to choose from: Newton (growth & blending), Curie (bloom & spice), Faraday (stability & depth), Feynman (rhythm & iron), Darwin (complexity & sulfur), Gauss (drift & water).

Game controller (standard Gamepad API)

  • Right stick — frequency / phase pad
  • Left stick — amplitude pad
  • Start — STOP (master to 0)
  • L1 / R1 — nudge Master Volume down / up
  • L2 / R2 — nudge Base Frequency down / up
  • X / Y / A / B — select active channel
  • D‑pad — adjust selected channel (Up/Down amplitude, Left/Right multiplier)
  • Paddles / L3 / R3 — nudge phase left / right

Tips

  • Always set Max caps before raising Master Volume.
  • In Biome mode, let the ecosystem run for a few minutes before adjusting — species populations shift naturally and so do the sensations.
  • Disconnect devices in‑app before powering them off.
